    • Chemical Bonds

    This sections will discuss the definition of a Chemical bond with in respect to ionic and covalent bonds.
    Chemical bonds- are the result of the interactions between the charged particle electrons and protons
    that compose atoms. When two element such as a Carbon (C) and Oxygen (O) form CO or CO2 as
    forming a compound through the bonding of electrons and protons.

    Ionic bonds- is when to atoms or elements lose or gain an electron during the undergoing of a chemical reaction.
    The chemical bond is typically between a metal and a none metal. Based on the outcome of the chemical reaction,
    the chemical bond can be a cations or anion.

    Cations- is when an element undergoes a chemical reaction, and the elements loses an electron, Causing the element to have a (+) charge.

    Anions- is when an element undergoes a chemical reaction, and the elements gains an electron, Causing the element to gain a (-) charge.

    Covalent bond- is when two or more none-metals atoms/elements undergoes a chemical reaction, where the electrons are shared between both elements lowering their potiental energy through the electrostatic interactions with the nuclei.
    Note that there is no lose or gain of electrons when a covalent bond is formed.

    Chemical formulas can be written in the three different ways: Molecular formula , Empirical formula , and Structural formula .

    Molecular formula - is the actual number of atoms of each element in a molecule of a compound.
    Example: C4H 8

    Empirical formula- is written similarly to molecular formula , however the Empirical formula find the greatest common factor between coefficients and reduced to it simplest form.
    Example:
    Given the Molecular formula → C4H8 → the greatest common factor of coefficient is 4.
    C4H8 converted to the Empirical formula → CH2.

    Structural formula- uses lines to reperesent covalent bonds to show how atoms in a molecule are connected or bonded to each other.
    Example: C4H8

    Molecular Compounds, Ionic Compounds, and Polyatomic Ions

    Atomic elements- are elements that exists in nature with single atoms. For Example, helium is composed with the helium atoms, aluminum is composed of aluminum atoms, and iron is composed of iron atoms.

    Molecular elements- are elements that exists as molecules- two or more atoms of the element are bonded together.
    Most molecular elements exist as diatomic molecules .

    Example of Diatomic Molecules :
    H2 → hydrogen is composed of H2 molecules.
    N2 → nitrogen is composed of N2 molecules.
    Cl2 → chlorine is composed of Cl2 molecules.

    Click to view all the Diatomic Molecules .

    Molecular compounds- are molecules of two covalently bonded nonmetals. For Example, H2O. Note that this means that there is no loss or gain of electrons between the bonded elements, instead the both elements share their electrons during bonding.

    Ionic compounds- are compounds that are bonded between one type of metal and one or more types of none-metals. There are two rules that are needed to identify if a given compound is an Ionic compound. In most cases, the first element of the compound need to be a metal that is cation(+), and the second element needs to be anion (-). The second rule is that charge count of the cation (+) needs to be the equalivent count of the anion (-) for the compound to charge neutral. Note that unlike Molecular compounds, Ionic compounds can experience a gain or loss of electrons during a chemical reaction.

    Example of an ionic compound:
    NaCl → Na+1 Cl-1
    pre-chemical reaction, Sodium (Na) reacts with Chlorine (Cl),
    Sodium (Na) contains 11 electrons, while Chlorine (Cl) contains 17 electron.
    Post chemical reaction Na+ + Cl- → NaCl
    Sodium (Na) contains 10 electron, Cholrine contains 18 electrons.
